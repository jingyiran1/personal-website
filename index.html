Interests · Tennis

Tennis & USTA

A sport that sparked a career, ignited a lifestyle, and never let go.

When Li Na became the first Asian player to win a Grand Slam title at the French Open in 2011, I was hooked instantly to this sport. Since then, I've watched hundreds of matches, online and in person. My favorite players are Li Na and Victoria Azarenka — two fierce competitors from the golden era of WTA tennis. Their hunger to win, their resilience under pressure, and their incredible personalities have inspired me in ways that go far beyond the sport.

And then there's this: my very first professional experience in data science started with tennis. When I was at CMU, I applied for the Data Analytics Intern role at the United States Tennis Association (USTA). After finishing a Tableau take-home challenge analyzing player sideline accuracy — leveraging everything I knew as a 10-year tennis fan — and sharing my story with tennis and data analytics in the interview, I knew that was my moment. I got the role, worked on the upcoming US Open, and built an ARIMA time-series forecasting model to predict US Open store sales. That internship was the very beginning of my data science career — and it started with a sport I loved.

Tennis also changed how I live. It pulled me into a world of movement — I started learning tennis, discovered a love for physical challenge, and never looked back. It was the beginning of a lifestyle built around energy, discipline, and showing up for myself every day.
